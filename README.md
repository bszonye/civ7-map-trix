# Map Trix 4.0.1
better map tools for Civilization VII

## better map tooltips
many small improvements to the new map tooltips:
- color highlighting for important terrain features
- new labels for rivers, bridges, and fresh water
- type icons for buildings and wonders
- new alerts for Temporary Fortifications, Dig Sites, and Jaguar Traps
- better friend-or-foe indicators
- improved layout and consistency with more compact text
- hotkey (Shift) to temporarily hide the tooltip

## settlement and unit lists
two new panels list every settlement in your empire and every unit on
the map.  click the list to visit a settlement or select a unit.

## better yield banner
the system bar adds new backgrounds to the empire yield banner, with
more legible text and color coding to match the tooltips.

## more lens layers
eight new decoration layers offer guidance and customization options.

- borders:  optionally turn off all borders for a more scenic map
- city limits:  outline each settlement instead of whole empires
- discoveries:  mark the location of goody huts and dig sites
- fortifications:  show which tiles are fortified and who controls them
- religion:  see exactly which tiles to visit with your missionaries
- roads:  overlay a map of the road and railroad network
- terrain:  highlight terrain features that block movement or vision
- wonders: show constructed and natural wonders on the map

activate layers in the minimap or with configurable shortcut keys.

## more lenses
new Commander and Religion lenses activate automatically when you select
appropriate units.  the Commander lens can optionally include commander,
military, and recon units.

## hotkeys
`Home` takes you to your capital city, on the map or the city screen.
(it opens and closes the city screen if you're already at the capital)

every map menu, lens, and decoration layer has a hotkey:
- `-` Lenses panel
- `0` Default lens
- `1` Settler lens
- `2` Continent lens
- `3` Trade lens
- `4` Appeal lens
- `5` Discovery lens
- `6` Religion lens
- `7` Commander lens
- `Shift+S` Settlements panel
- `Shift+U` Units panel
- `Shift+B` Borders
- `Shift+C` City Limits
- `Shift+D` Discoveries
- `Shift+F` Fortifications
- `Shift+R` Religion
- `Shift+X` Roads
- `Shift+T` Terrain
- `Shift+W` Wonders
- `Shift+Q` Conquest
